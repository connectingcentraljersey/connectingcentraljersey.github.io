About

Connecting Central Jersey (CCJ) was founded in 2017 by two (then) high school sophomores, Matthew Ye and Peter Wu, both students at the West Windsor-Plainsboro School District. It started off as a programming class led by two high school sophomores at the local library, but we enjoyed teaching these classes so much that we decided to do it a second time...and then a third. Now, CCJ seeks to offer free computer-related classes to all people, regardless of age or skill. Both Peter and Matthew earned 5 (max score) on the Advanced Placement Computer Science exams and are certified USA Computing Olympiad Gold Division.

Services

We believe that digital literacy is a necessity in the twenty-first century, and that everyone is entitled to an understanding of this ubiquitous component of our daily lives.

Programming Instruction/Tutoring

Ever since the summer of 2017, we have been offering programming classes at the Mercer County West Windsor Library, offering middle school students free instruction on the basics of object-oriented programming in Java. We have repeated these sessions throughout the 2017-2018 school year, and into the summer of 2018.

Technological Literacy/Support

As technologically-fluent users in the 21st century world, we hope to assist or help further facilitate usage of technology for all ages, especially as the next innovations surface faster and faster.
